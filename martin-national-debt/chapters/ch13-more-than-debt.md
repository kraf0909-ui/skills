# Chapter 13: Is There More Than the National Debt?

## Core Idea
The headline National Debt figure captures only a fraction of the government's true financial position. Whole of Government Accounts, the Fiscal Sustainability Report, and analysis of private-sector debt reveal a much larger — and more complex — picture of national indebtedness.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **The Four Distortions Framework** (expanded):
  1. Domestic debt is internal redistribution, not net loss
  2. Official debt understates future liabilities (pensions, PFI, nuclear decommissioning)
  3. Governments have assets that offset liabilities
  4. Private-sector debt interacts with public debt
- **Whole of Government Accounts (WGA)**: Treats government as a corporation, including assets and liabilities beyond the National Debt. Net liabilities in 2015–16 were £1,986 billion vs. official debt of £1,603 billion.
- **The Fiscal Sustainability Report (FSR)**: The OBR's 50-year projection under unchanged policy. In 2017, it projected debt/GDP rising to 234% by 2066–67, driven by aging and healthcare costs.

## Key Concepts
- **Public-sector pension entitlements**: £1,425 billion in WGA — nearly as large as the official debt
- **PFI (Private Finance Initiative)**: Off-balance-sheet infrastructure finance with total commitments of ~£200 billion
- **Nuclear decommissioning**: £182 billion liability, payable over more than a century
- **Quantitative Easing complications**: The Bank of England holds ~25% of gilts, creating accounting and policy complexities

## Mental Models
- Think of headline debt as the visible tip of an iceberg; the submerged mass is often larger
- Use WGA as a complement, not replacement, for debt analysis: it reveals different things but has its own limitations

## Anti-patterns
- **Treating WGA as precise**: Asset valuations are uncertain, and the government boundary is contestable
- **Adding all liabilities together without regard to timing**: A £182 billion nuclear liability over 120 years is very different from £182 billion due tomorrow

## Key Takeaways
1. WGA net liabilities (£1,986bn in 2015–16) exceeded official National Debt (£1,603bn)
2. Public-sector pension entitlements were the largest non-debt liability (~£1,425bn)
3. PFI was a costly accounting manoeuvre: higher interest costs in exchange for off-balance-sheet presentation
4. The OBR's FSR projected debt/GDP reaching 234% by 2066–67 under unchanged policy
5. The "unchanged policy" assumption is unrealistic but serves as a warning, not a forecast
