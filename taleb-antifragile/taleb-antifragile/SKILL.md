## Core Frameworks & Mental Models

### The Triad: Fragile — Robust — Antifragile
The central classification system. Use it to analyze any system:
- **Fragile**: harmed by stress, volatility, and disorder (damocles, crystal glass)
- **Robust**: resists stress but does not improve (phoenix, returns to same state)
- **Antifragile**: benefits from stress, grows stronger with shocks (hydra, evolution)

**Rule**: Seek antifragile positions. Avoid fragile ones. Robust is acceptable but not ideal.

### Optionality
The property of having more options than obligations. The ultimate source of antifragility.
- **Key property**: limited downside, unlimited upside
- **How to acquire**: keep slack, avoid commitments, build skills that transfer
- **Life is long gamma**: position yourself to benefit from volatility

### The Barbell Strategy
Bifurcated exposure: extreme safety + extreme risk, nothing in the mediocre middle.
- **Origin**: Seneca kept most wealth in safe land, risked a small portion in trade
- **Application**: 90% safe + 10% wild; avoid "balanced" moderate-risk portfolios
- **Why it works**: eliminates catastrophic downside while preserving upside

### Via Negativa (The Way of Subtraction)
Improvement through removal rather than addition.
- **In medicine**: stop harmful treatments before adding new ones
- **In life**: use "not to do" lists; avoid stupidity rather than pursue brilliance
- **In knowledge**: subtractive knowledge (what doesn't work) > additive knowledge (theories)

### Skin in the Game
Having a personal stake in the outcomes of your actions. The ethical foundation of antifragile systems.
- **Test**: Does the advice-giver bear the cost if wrong?
- **Red flag**: Fragilistas give advice without consequences
- **Rule**: Favor artisans over bureaucrats; practitioners over theorists

### Iatrogenics (Harm by Healers)
Intervention that causes more harm than good.
- **First principle**: Do no harm (Primum non nocere)
- **Second principle**: In complex organic systems, intervention risk is highest
- **Fabian procrastination**: strategic delay as optionality

### The Turkey Problem
False confidence from a long stable period. The turkey fed for 1,000 days is most confident right before Thanksgiving.
- **Warning signs**: increasing confidence, dismissal of tail risks, "this time is different"
- **Antidote**: stress-test systems; seek evidence of genuine resilience

### Lindy Effect
For non-perishable things (ideas, technologies, books), expected remaining life increases with age.
- **Heuristic**: old > new; be skeptical of neomania
- **Thresholds**: <5 years = experimental; >50 years = proven; >500 years = very high confidence

### Convexity & Jensen's Inequality
Convex exposures benefit from volatility; concave exposures are harmed.
- **Convex (smiles)**: unlimited upside, bounded downside → seek
- **Concave (frowns)**: bounded upside, unlimited downside → avoid
- **Application**: investment portfolios, medical interventions, political systems

### Fat Tony's Wisdom
Practical intelligence over theoretical knowledge.
- **Sucker vs. Nonsucker**: suckers focus on probability; nonsuckers focus on payoff asymmetry
- **Portfolio rule**: "Don't tell me what you think; tell me what you have in your portfolio"
- **Positioning over prediction**: be ready for multiple outcomes, don't forecast
