## Core Frameworks & Mental Models

### 差序格局（The Differential Mode of Association）
The central concept of the book. Chinese social structure is not like a bundle of sticks (团体格局) but like ripples in water — centered on the self (己), extending outward in circles of decreasing intimacy and obligation.

**Key implications**:
- No clear boundary between public and private; they are relative
- Morality is contextual, depending on the relationship to the actor
- Self-ism (自我主义), not individualism — the self is the center, not an equal member of a group
- The concept explains why "selfishness" is so prevalent in Chinese society — it's a structural feature, not a moral failing

### 四种权力类型（Four Types of Power）
Fei's framework for analyzing power in any society:
- **横暴权力 (Coercive Power)**: From social conflict, exploitative, top-down (e.g., imperial authority)
- **同意权力 (Consent Power)**: From social cooperation, contractual, based on division of labor (e.g., democracy)
- **教化权力/长老权力 (Educative/Elder Power)**: From social succession, paternalistic, based on tradition (e.g., father-son, master-disciple)
- **时势权力 (Situational Power)**: From rapid social change, exercised by "cultural heroes" who propose new solutions (e.g., revolutionaries, reformers)

**Application**: Identify which power type dominates in a given social situation to understand its logic.

### 礼治秩序（Ritual Order）
Rural Chinese society maintains order not through law (法治) or arbitrary rule (人治), but through 礼 — socially accepted norms maintained by tradition and education.

**Key features**:
- People follow rituals voluntarily, out of internalized respect
- Maintained by tradition, not state power
- Effective only when society changes slowly
- The ideal state is 无讼 (no litigation) — disputes resolved through mediation and education

### 从欲望到需要（From Desire to Need）
A milestone in social transition:
- **Desire (欲望)**: Culturally shaped wants that guide behavior in traditional society; unconsciously aligned with survival conditions
- **Need (需要)**: Consciously recognized conditions for survival; requires rational planning
- The shift from desire to need marks the transition from traditional to modern society
- In modern society, "knowledge is power" because planning requires knowledge

### 乡土社会 vs 现代社会
| Dimension | Rural Society | Modern Society |
|-----------|--------------|----------------|
| Structure | 差序格局 | 团体格局 |
| Order | 礼治 | 法治 |
| Power | 长老权力 | 时势权力 |
| Bond | 血缘 | 地缘/契约 |
| Guidance | 欲望 | 需要 |
| Culture | 亚普罗式 | 浮士德式 |

## Scope & Limits

This skill covers the book content only. The book was written in the 1940s based on observations of rural China. While the core concepts (especially 差序格局) remain highly influential, some social conditions have changed significantly. Use with awareness of historical context.

**Related works by Fei Xiaotong**:
- 《江村经济》(Peasant Life in China) — detailed village study
- 《生育制度》(The Reproductive System) — family and kinship
- 《乡土重建》(Rural Reconstruction) — applied sociology
