# Chapter 13: The Self-Inflicted Wounds

## Core Idea
The Great Depression was not caused by external shocks or market forces beyond human control. It was caused by the rigid application of orthodox economic doctrine — the gold standard, balanced budgets, and liquidationism — in the face of a crisis that demanded flexibility. The wounds were self-inflicted by policymakers who clung to impeccable theories that were catastrophically wrong for the circumstances.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Liquidationism as Orthodoxy**: Treasury Secretary Andrew Mellon and others believed the depression was a necessary purging of excesses. "Liquidate labor, liquidate stocks, liquidate the farmers, liquidate real estate." This was not policy failure but policy success — by the lights of orthodoxy.
- **The Gold Standard as Straitjacket**: Countries that clung to gold longest (the US, France, Germany) suffered most. Countries that abandoned gold earliest (Britain, Scandinavia, Japan) recovered first.
- **The Self-Inflicted Wound**: The depression was not an act of God but a policy choice — or rather, a series of policy choices driven by adherence to orthodox doctrine.

## Key Concepts
- **Liquidationism**: The belief that depressions purge the economy of excesses and should not be interfered with
- **Hoover's response**: Initially activist (Reconstruction Finance Corporation), but constrained by balanced-budget orthodoxy
- **The Revenue Act of 1932**: A sharp tax increase in the depths of the depression, worsening the downturn
- **Bank failures**: Thousands of banks failed because the Federal Reserve refused to act as lender of last resort
- **The gold standard constraint**: Prevented monetary expansion and forced deflationary policies

## Mental Models
- Think of orthodoxy as a trap: the more rigorously it is applied, the worse the outcome
- Use the self-inflicted wound framework when evaluating policy responses to crisis: is the policy helping or harming?

## Anti-patterns
- **Attributing the depression to "market forces"**: It was caused by policy choices, not by inevitable economic laws
- **Assuming Hoover did nothing**: He did much, but what he did was often counterproductive by orthodox design

## Key Takeaways
1. The Great Depression was caused by the rigid application of orthodox doctrine
2. Liquidationism — the belief that depressions purge excesses — guided policy
3. The gold standard prevented monetary expansion and forced deflation
4. The Federal Reserve failed to act as lender of last resort
5. Countries that abandoned gold earliest recovered first
