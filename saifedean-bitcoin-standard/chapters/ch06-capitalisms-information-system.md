# Chapter 6: Capitalism's Information System

## Core Idea
Prices are the information system of the market economy. Sound money produces accurate price signals because it is not subject to manipulation. Fiat money distorts prices — particularly interest rates — leading to malinvestment, business cycles, and financial crises. Central planning of money (central banking) is as destructive as central planning of any other good.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Prices as Information**: In a market economy, prices convey information about scarcity, demand, and opportunity cost. This information is essential for coordinating the actions of millions of economic actors. Sound money preserves the integrity of this information; fiat money corrupts it.
- **The Interest Rate as the Most Important Price**: The interest rate coordinates consumption across time. When central banks manipulate interest rates, they destroy the information content of this price, leading to systematic misallocation of capital.
- **Austrian Business Cycle Theory**: Artificially low interest rates (caused by monetary expansion) encourage investment in projects that are not economically viable at natural rates. When the monetary expansion slows, these projects are revealed as malinvestments, causing recessions.

## Key Concepts
- **Price signals**: The information conveyed by market prices about relative scarcity and value
- **Interest rate**: The price of time; the premium for present goods over future goods
- **Malinvestment**: Investment in projects that appear profitable only because of distorted price signals
- **Business cycle**: The boom-bust pattern caused by monetary expansion and contraction
- **Capital market socialism**: The central planning of interest rates and credit allocation by central banks

## Mental Models
- Think of central banking as price control for the most important price in the economy
- Use Austrian business cycle theory to predict where monetary expansion will create malinvestment

## Anti-patterns
- **Assuming central banks can fine-tune the economy**: They lack the information to set the "correct" interest rate
- **Blaming markets for crises caused by monetary policy**: The 2008 crisis was caused by Fed policy, not by market failure

## Key Takeaways
1. Prices are the information system of the market economy
2. Sound money preserves accurate price signals; fiat money distorts them
3. The interest rate is the most important price; its manipulation causes malinvestment
4. Austrian business cycle theory explains the boom-bust pattern of fiat economies
5. Central banking is a form of capital market socialism — central planning of credit
