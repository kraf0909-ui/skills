# Chapter 1: Money

## Core Idea
Money is not a creation of the state but the most marketable commodity — the good with the highest salability across space, scale, and time. It emerges spontaneously through market exchange, not through government decree. A good becomes money because it wins the competition for universal acceptability, not because it is declared legal tender.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Salability as the Essence of Money**: Money is the most salable good — the one most readily accepted in exchange. Salability has three dimensions: across space (transportability), across scale (divisibility), and across time (durability/store of value).
- **Monetary Competition**: Money emerges through a market process of competition among goods. The good that best satisfies the properties of money (scarcity, durability, divisibility, recognisability, portability) becomes the monetary standard.
- **The Regression Theorem** (Mises): Money must originate as a commodity with non-monetary use value before it can become a medium of exchange. This explains why historical moneys (gold, silver, salt, cattle) had intrinsic utility before they became money.

## Key Concepts
- **Medium of exchange**: A good acquired not for consumption but for trading for other goods
- **Unit of account**: The common measure in which prices are quoted
- **Store of value**: The ability to preserve purchasing power across time
- **Salability**: The ease with which a good can be sold at the prevailing market price
- **Network effects**: Money becomes more useful as more people use it

## Mental Models
- Think of money as the winner of a market competition for universal acceptability, not as a government invention
- Use the three dimensions of salability (space, scale, time) to evaluate any potential money

## Anti-patterns
- **Chartalist fallacy**: Believing money exists because the state declares it; the state can declare but cannot compel acceptance
- **Ignoring network effects**: A better money cannot displace an incumbent unless the switching costs are overcome

## Key Takeaways
1. Money is the most salable good, emerging through market exchange
2. Salability has three dimensions: space, scale, and time
3. Money must originate as a commodity with non-monetary value (Regression Theorem)
4. Network effects make monetary standards persistent but not immutable
5. Government decree cannot create money; it can only exploit existing monetary networks
