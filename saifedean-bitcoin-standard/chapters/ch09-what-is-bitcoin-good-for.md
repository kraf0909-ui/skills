# Chapter 9: What Is Bitcoin Good For?

## Core Idea
Bitcoin's primary and most important use is as a store of value — a way to preserve purchasing power across time without counterparty risk. Beyond this, it enables individual monetary sovereignty, censorship-resistant international settlement, and potentially a global unit of account. These functions are not theoretical; they are already being used by people in countries with failing currencies and capital controls.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Store of Value as Primary Function**: Bitcoin's most important use is not as a medium of exchange for daily transactions but as a long-term store of value. Its volatility is a feature of its early adoption phase, not a permanent flaw; as the market deepens, volatility will decrease.
- **Individual Sovereignty**: Bitcoin allows individuals to hold and transfer wealth without relying on any institution — bank, government, or corporation. The private key is the ultimate form of property rights.
- **International Settlement**: Bitcoin enables final settlement of large-value transactions across borders in hours, without intermediaries, without permission, and without counterparty risk. This is a profound improvement on the correspondent banking system.
- **Global Unit of Account**: As Bitcoin's market capitalisation grows and volatility decreases, it has the potential to become a global unit of account — the standard against which other goods and currencies are measured.

## Key Concepts
- **Store of value**: An asset that preserves purchasing power across time
- **Individual sovereignty**: The ability to control one's own wealth without institutional intermediaries
- **Final settlement**: Irreversible transfer of ownership without counterparty risk
- **Censorship resistance**: The inability of any authority to block or reverse transactions
- **Unit of account**: The standard in which prices are quoted

## Mental Models
- Think of Bitcoin as digital property — the first form of property that can be owned outright without any institutional framework
- Use the sovereignty framework when evaluating financial systems: who has control over your money?

## Anti-patterns
- **Dismissing Bitcoin because it is volatile**: Volatility is a feature of early adoption, not a permanent flaw
- **Demanding immediate medium-of-exchange functionality**: Money historically evolves from store of value to medium of exchange, not the reverse

## Key Takeaways
1. Bitcoin's primary function is store of value, not daily medium of exchange
2. It enables individual monetary sovereignty — ownership without institutions
3. International settlement is faster, cheaper, and more final than correspondent banking
4. As market depth increases, volatility will decrease and unit-of-account function will emerge
5. These are not theoretical benefits; they are already being used in failing-currency countries
