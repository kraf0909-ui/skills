# Chapter 8: Digital Money

## Core Idea
Bitcoin is the first digital money that achieves the monetary properties of gold — scarcity, durability, divisibility, recognisability, portability — while adding digital advantages: perfect portability, perfect divisibility, perfect verifiability, and programmatic scarcity. Its 21 million supply cap and difficulty adjustment make it the hardest money ever invented.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Bitcoin as Digital Gold**: Bitcoin combines the monetary properties that made gold the historical standard with digital advantages. It is scarcer than gold (fixed supply vs. slowly increasing), more divisible, more portable, and more verifiable.
- **Programmatic Scarcity**: Bitcoin's 21 million cap is enforced by consensus rules, not by physical geology. The halving schedule (every 210,000 blocks, roughly 4 years) reduces new issuance by half, causing stock-to-flow to rise toward infinity.
- **Difficulty Adjustment**: Every 2,016 blocks (roughly 2 weeks), Bitcoin adjusts its mining difficulty to maintain a 10-minute average block time. This is the mechanism that ensures stable issuance regardless of mining power.
- **The Blockchain as Triple-Entry Bookkeeping**: The blockchain creates an immutable, publicly verifiable ledger of all transactions. This eliminates the need for trusted third parties in settlement.

## Key Concepts
- **21 million cap**: The fixed maximum supply of Bitcoin, enforced by consensus
- **Halving**: The 50% reduction in block reward every 210,000 blocks
- **Difficulty adjustment**: The automatic recalibration of mining difficulty to maintain block time
- **Proof of work**: The energy-intensive process that secures the blockchain and makes it costly to attack
- **Private key / public key**: The cryptographic pair that enables ownership and transfer of bitcoins
- **Blockchain**: The distributed, immutable ledger of all Bitcoin transactions

## Mental Models
- Think of Bitcoin's difficulty adjustment as the digital equivalent of gold's geological scarcity: both make production costly and predictable
- Use stock-to-flow to compare Bitcoin's hardness to gold and other assets over time

## Anti-patterns
- **Assuming Bitcoin is "backed by nothing"**: It is backed by proof of work, cryptographic security, and network consensus — which is more verifiable than gold's physical properties
- **Ignoring the difficulty adjustment**: This is the critical innovation that makes Bitcoin's issuance schedule predictable regardless of demand or technology

## Key Takeaways
1. Bitcoin combines gold's monetary properties with digital advantages
2. The 21 million cap and halving schedule make Bitcoin's stock-to-flow rise over time
3. Difficulty adjustment ensures stable issuance regardless of mining power
4. Proof of work makes the blockchain costly to attack and easy to verify
5. Bitcoin is the hardest money ever invented — its stock-to-flow will exceed gold's and continue rising
