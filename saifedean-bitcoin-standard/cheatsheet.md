# Cheatsheet

## Decision Rules

### Is a good suitable as money?
- **Assess across five properties**: scarcity, durability, divisibility, recognisability, portability
- **Prioritise hardness**: High stock-to-flow is the most important property for long-term monetary value
- **Reject if easily producible**: Goods with low production costs cannot sustain monetary value

### Is a monetary standard "sound"?
- **Sound when**: supply is constrained by market forces or algorithm, not by political discretion
- **Unsound when**: a central authority can increase supply arbitrarily
- **Test**: Has the money's purchasing power been stable or increasing over decades?

### When to prefer Bitcoin over gold?
- **Prefer Bitcoin when**: perfect portability, perfect divisibility, verifiability, or programmatic scarcity are needed
- **Prefer gold when**: physical tangibility, millennia of track record, or non-digital durability are required
- **Reality**: Both are hard money; Bitcoin is harder in some dimensions, gold in others

### Is an altcoin a serious monetary competitor?
- **Serious when**: it has Bitcoin-level decentralisation, immutability, and proof-of-work security
- **Not serious when**: it sacrifices decentralisation for speed, governance, or features
- **Rule**: Money's primary requirement is trustworthiness, not convenience

### How to evaluate central bank policy?
- **Ask**: Is the interest rate being manipulated below its natural level?
- **If yes**: expect malinvestment, asset bubbles, and eventual recession
- **Ask**: Who receives new money first, and who last?
- **If unequal**: the Cantillon Effect is transferring wealth upward

## Decision Tree: Choosing a Store of Value

```
Is physical tangibility essential?
  YES → Gold or other precious metals
  NO → Is programmatic scarcity and verifiability important?
          YES → Bitcoin
          NO → Is institutional custody acceptable?
                    YES → Traditional financial assets
                    NO → Bearer instruments or cash
```

## Thresholds & Defaults

- **Gold stock-to-flow**: ~60
- **Bitcoin stock-to-flow (2024)**: ~120 (post-halving)
- **Bitcoin stock-to-flow (2140)**: Infinite (no new issuance)
- **Bitcoin block time target**: 10 minutes
- **Bitcoin difficulty adjustment**: Every 2,016 blocks (~2 weeks)
- **Bitcoin halving interval**: Every 210,000 blocks (~4 years)
- **Bitcoin total supply cap**: 21,000,000
- **US dollar purchasing power loss since 1913**: ~95%
- **Byzantine bezant purity maintenance**: 600+ years
- **La Belle Epoque**: 1871–1914
- **Bretton Woods**: 1944–1971

## Tells & Smells

- **If a government claims to manage money for the public good**: inspect its track record of debasement
- **If a monetary system allows arbitrary supply increases**: it will be inflated; the only question is when and how fast
- **If an altcoin promises Bitcoin's features with better speed**: it has likely sacrificed decentralisation
- **If someone says "blockchain, not Bitcoin"**: they misunderstand where the value lies
- **If Bitcoin is declared dead**: it has likely just become stronger (antifragility)
- **If interest rates are held below inflation for years**: malinvestment and asset bubbles are building
- **If monetary policy is described as "stimulative"**: ask who is being stimulated and who is paying
- **If a currency is backed by "the full faith and credit" of a government**: inspect the government's fiscal position
