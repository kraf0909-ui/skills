# Chapter 15: 求取共识并坚持

## Core Idea
Conflict is essential for testing principles and finding the best solutions. The goal is not to eliminate disagreement but to have a clear, efficient process for resolving it — maintaining relationships while reaching the best decisions.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Principled Conflict Resolution**: Resolve disagreements by referring to shared principles
  - When to use: Any significant disagreement
  - How: Identify the relevant principle; apply it; if principles conflict, escalate
- **Two-Minute Rule**: Let people speak for two minutes without interruption
  - When to use: Meetings, debates, discussions
  - How: Enforce no-interruption periods; use timers
- **Jazz Band Collaboration**: Great collaboration is like jazz — structured improvisation
  - When to use: Team projects, creative work, problem-solving
  - How: Establish clear structure and roles; allow freedom within that structure

## Key Concepts
- **Conflict as test**: Disagreements test whether principles are clear and shared
- **Resolution paths**: Discussion → escalation → decision → compliance
- **Decision rights**: Clarify who decides what after input is gathered
- **1 + 1 = 3**: Synergy from combining different perspectives
- **Small teams**: 3-5 people are more efficient than 20

## Mental Models
- Think of disagreement as a treasure hunt — the truth is often found through conflict
- Use "decision rights" to prevent consensus paralysis — not everyone decides, everyone contributes
- View great collaboration as jazz — individual excellence within shared structure

## Anti-patterns
- **Conflict avoidance**: Avoiding necessary disagreements to preserve harmony
- **Consensus paralysis**: Requiring everyone to agree before acting
- **Talking without deciding**: Endless discussion without resolution
- **Majority rule without merit**: Voting without weighting by credibility
- **Post-decision undermining**: Continuing to oppose after a decision is made

## Key Takeaways
1. Conflict is essential for testing principles and finding best solutions
2. Have a clear process: discuss → escalate if needed → decide → comply
3. Use the two-minute rule to ensure everyone is heard
4. Clarify decision rights — not everyone decides, everyone contributes
5. Great collaboration is like jazz — structured improvisation
6. Once decided, everyone must comply, even if they disagree
7. If you can't resolve fundamental value differences, consider ending the relationship
