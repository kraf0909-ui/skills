# Chapter 3: 我的低谷 (1979-1982)

## Core Idea
Dalio's catastrophic failure in 1982 — predicting a depression that didn't come — taught him humility, the value of radical open-mindedness, and the foundation of what would become "pain + reflection = progress." This failure led directly to the creation of Bridgewater's idea meritocracy culture.

## Frameworks Introduced
- **Pain + Reflection = Progress**: The core learning loop
  - When to use: After any failure, setback, or painful experience
  - How: Record the pain when it happens, reflect when calm, extract principles
- **Radical Open-Mindedness Origin**: "How do I know I'm right?"
  - When to use: When you feel certain about something important
  - How: Find the smartest people who disagree, understand their reasoning
- **Idea Meritocracy Origin**: Weight opinions by credibility, not hierarchy
  - When to use: Group decisions where expertise varies
  - How: Track people's decision records, weight their opinions accordingly

## Key Concepts
- **Silver market collapse (1980)**: Bunker Hunt's corner failed, silver fell from $50 to $11
- **1982 non-depression**: Fed stimulus worked, economy recovered without hyperinflation
- **Credibility-weighted decisions**: The origin of Bridgewater's decision system
- **Balance of risk and return**: The "Holy Grail of investing" — finding low-risk, high-return positions

## Mental Models
- Think of failure as a puzzle that contains a gem (principle) if you can extract it
- Use "How do I know I'm right?" as a default question before acting on strong beliefs
- View yourself as a machine that can be redesigned based on feedback

## Anti-patterns
- **Overconfidence**: "I can say with absolute certainty that a soft landing will not occur"
- **Ignoring disconfirming evidence**: Sticking to a thesis after the world changes
- **Ego-driven decision making**: Needing to be right rather than finding the right answer
- **All-or-nothing thinking**: Assuming you must choose between high risk/high return and low risk/low return

## Worked Example
Dalio's 1982 mistake:
- Thesis: Debt crisis → depression (75% probability)
- Position: Long gold, long bonds as hedge
- What happened: Fed stimulus + capital flight to US → strong dollar, low inflation, economic boom
- Why wrong: Missed that dollar-denominated debt can be restructured with government help; missed that simultaneous global stimulus can create growth without inflation
- Lesson: "I was an arrogant jerk who was totally and completely wrong"

## Key Takeaways
1. The biggest failures contain the most valuable lessons
2. "How do I know I'm right?" is the most important question
3. Find smart people who disagree with you and understand their reasoning
4. Your ego is the biggest barrier to learning
5. There is almost always a path to get both low risk and high return — keep looking
