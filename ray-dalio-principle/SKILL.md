## Core Frameworks & Mental Models

### 1. Radical Truth + Radical Transparency (极度求真 + 极度透明)
Use when building trust, making hard decisions, or designing organizational culture.
- Radical truth means seeking accurate understanding of reality, not what you wish to be true.
- Radical transparency means making almost everything visible to almost everyone.
- Combined with credibility-weighted decision-making, this forms "idea meritocracy."

### 2. Idea Meritocracy (创意择优)
Use when making group decisions or building decision systems.
- The best idea wins, regardless of who proposes it.
- Weight opinions by credibility: people who have successfully solved a problem 3+ times have higher credibility.
- Requires radical truth and transparency to function.

### 3. The Five-Step Process (五步流程)
Use for any personal or organizational goal achievement.
1. Have clear goals
2. Identify problems and don't tolerate them
3. Diagnose problems to get at root causes
4. Design a plan
5. Push through to completion
- Know which step you tend to fail at and strengthen it.

### 4. Pain + Reflection = Progress (痛苦 + 反思 = 进步)
Use when facing setbacks, failures, or emotional difficulty.
- Pain is a signal that something important needs to be learned.
- Reflect on pain when calm, not while experiencing it.
- Build a feedback loop: experience → reflect → extract principle → apply.

### 5. The Machine Metaphor (机器视角)
Use when managing yourself, others, or organizations.
- View yourself as a machine operating within a larger machine.
- Distinguish between you as the machine designer and you as the machine worker.
- Compare outcomes to goals, then redesign the machine to improve results.

### 6. Believability-Weighted Decisions (可信度加权决策)
Use when evaluating opinions or making decisions with others.
- Not all opinions are equally valuable.
- Highly believable people have: (a) at least 3 successful experiences solving the problem, and (b) strong causal reasoning.
- When you disagree with highly believable people, ask "How do I know I'm right?"

### 7. Radical Open-Mindedness (头脑极度开放)
Use when you feel strongly about something or are in disagreement.
- Your ego and blind spots are the two biggest barriers to good decisions.
- Seek out smart people who disagree with you and understand their reasoning.
- Suspend judgment to truly see from another's perspective.

### 8. People Are Wired Differently (人与人大不相同)
Use when hiring, team building, or understanding conflicts.
- Different brain wiring means different strengths and weaknesses.
- Key dimensions: introvert/extrovert, intuitive/sensing, thinking/feeling, planning/discovering.
- Archetypes: Creators, Advancers, Refiners, Executors, Flexors.
- Shapers can go from vision to realization.

### 9. Meaningful Work + Meaningful Relationships (有意义的工作和人际关系)
Use when making life or career decisions.
- These are Dalio's two primary life goals, valued equally.
- Money is secondary; it enables but does not replace these.
- They reinforce each other, especially in a radically transparent environment.

### 10. Culture + People = Organization (文化 + 人 = 机构)
Use when building or transforming organizations.
- An organization is a machine with two parts: culture and people.
- Great culture: doesn't hide problems, solves them openly, encourages imagination.
- Great people: high character (truth-seeking, open) + high capability.
