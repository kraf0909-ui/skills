# Cheatsheet

## Decision Rules

### When facing a major decision:
1. Ask: "How do I know I'm right?"
2. Find the smartest people who disagree with you
3. Understand their reasoning before defending your view
4. Weight opinions by credibility (track record + reasoning), not confidence
5. Calculate expected value when probabilities are involved
6. Consider second- and third-order consequences

### When you feel certain about something:
- That's when you're most at risk of being wrong
- Seek out credible dissenters immediately
- Suspend judgment and genuinely try to see their perspective
- Ask: "What would convince me I'm wrong?"

### When evaluating someone's opinion:
| Factor | High Credibility | Low Credibility |
|--------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Track record | 3+ successful experiences | No relevant experience |
| Reasoning | Logical, causal, evidence-based | Emotional, superficial |
| Domain | Specific to the problem | General or unrelated |

## The Five-Step Process (Quick Reference)

| Step | Question | Common Failure |
|------|----------|----------------|
| 1. Goals | What do I truly want? | Confusing goals with desires |
| 2. Problems | What's in my way? | Tolerating problems |
| 3. Diagnosis | Why is this happening? | Stopping at symptoms |
| 4. Plan | What's my script? | Vague or unrealistic plans |
| 5. Execution | Am I doing what I planned? | Procrastination, distraction |

**If stuck, ask: Which step do I most often fail at? Strengthen that one.**

## Pain + Reflection = Progress (Emergency Protocol)

1. **Feel it**: Don't suppress the pain
2. **Record it**: Write down what happened while fresh
3. **Wait**: Don't reflect while emotional
4. **Analyze**: What principle was violated? What would I do differently?
5. **Apply**: Use the extracted principle next time

## Organizational Health Check

| Signal | Healthy | Unhealthy |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| Disagreements | Open, frequent, resolved | Hidden, suppressed, festering |
| Errors | Admitted, analyzed, learned from | Hidden, blamed, repeated |
| Information | Broadly shared | Hoarded, politicized |
| Feedback | Direct, specific, caring | Indirect, vague, or absent |
| Decisions | Based on logic and credibility | Based on hierarchy or politics |

## Hiring Filter

**Reject if any are missing:**
1. Values fit (truth-seeking, open, mission-aligned)
2. Ability (can learn and adapt)
3. Skills (can do the job — teachable if ability is high)

**Red flags:**
- Can't explain their reasoning
- Defensive about past failures
- No questions for you
- Overly focused on compensation vs. mission

## Meeting Rules

1. Two-minute rule: Let people speak for 2 minutes without interruption
2. Clarify who decides before the discussion begins
3. Distinguish "by-above" (strategic) from "by-below" (tactical)
4. If disagreement persists after thorough discussion, escalate or vote
5. Once decided, everyone supports the decision

## When to Escalate

| Situation | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Principled disagreement unresolved after thorough discussion | Escalate to higher authority |
| Decision needed urgently | Decision maker decides with input, not consensus |
| Values conflict | Consider whether relationship should continue |
| Repeated same error | Systemic problem — redesign the machine |

## Personal Development

### Know your weaknesses:
- Ask others what they see
- Review your error patterns
- Identify which of the five steps you fail at most

### Build around weaknesses:
- Don't expect to fundamentally change
- Find people who are strong where you are weak
- Create systems and habits that compensate

### Daily practice:
- Meditate (creates space between stimulus and response)
- Record decisions and outcomes
- Review weekly: What did I learn? What principles emerged?

## Key Thresholds & Defaults

- **18 months**: Typical time to adapt to radical transparency culture
- **3+ experiences**: Minimum track record for high credibility on a topic
- **80/20**: Focus on the 20% that drives 80% of outcomes
- **5 Whys**: Standard root cause analysis depth
- **2 minutes**: Default uninterrupted speaking time in discussions
