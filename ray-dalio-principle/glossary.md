# Glossary

**80/20 Rule (Pareto Principle)** — 80% of outcomes come from 20% of causes. Focus on the vital few. (Ch 10)

**Alpha (阿尔法)** — Return generated from skill-based bets, separate from market return (beta). (Ch 4)

**Alpha Overlay (阿尔法叠加)** — Investment approach that separates passive market exposure (beta) from active skill-based bets (alpha). (Ch 4)

**Base Rate (基础比率)** — Historical frequency of an event, used as starting point for probability estimates. (Ch 10)

**Baseball Card (棒球卡)** — Profile of an individual's strengths, weaknesses, and characteristics, used for team building and role matching. (Ch 18)

**Believability (可信度)** — Measure of a person's track record and reasoning quality on a specific topic. (Ch 8, 16)

**Believability-Weighted Decision (可信度加权决策)** — Decision-making where opinions are weighted by the credibility of the person offering them. (Ch 3, 16)

**Beta (贝塔)** — Market return that comes from passive exposure to an asset class. (Ch 4)

**Blind Spot (思维盲点)** — Natural inability to see certain perspectives due to individual brain wiring. (Ch 8)

**By-Above / By-Below (基线以上/基线以下)** — Distinguishing high-level strategic discussions from tactical implementation details. (Ch 10)

**Cost-Averaging (成本平均策略)** — Investing fixed amounts at regular intervals regardless of price. Dalio observed this as a flawed conventional approach. (Ch 1)

**Creator (创造者)** — Personality archetype: generates new ideas and original concepts. (Ch 9)

**Daily Observations (每日观察)** — Dalio's daily market commentary, started in the late 1970s and continued for decades. (Ch 4)

**Ego Barrier (自我意识障碍)** — Brain's defense mechanism that prevents seeing one's own weaknesses and mistakes. (Ch 8)

**Evolution (进化)** — The most powerful force in the universe; the process of adaptation and improvement through trial and error. (Ch 6)

**Expected Value (预期价值)** — Probability-weighted average of all possible outcomes; key decision-making tool. (Ch 10)

**Executor (贯彻者)** — Personality archetype: ensures plans are completed and details are handled. (Ch 9)

**Five-Step Process (五步流程)** — Goal achievement framework: goals → problems → diagnosis → plan → execution. (Ch 7)

**Flexor (变通者)** — Personality archetype: adapts to fill gaps between other roles. (Ch 9)

**Higher-Level You (较高层次的你)** — The rational, reflective part of yourself that can design and improve your "machine." (Ch 6)

**Holy Grail of Investing (投资的圣杯)** — Finding a way to achieve high returns with low risk through proper diversification and system design. (Ch 3)

**Hyperrealism (超级现实主义)** — Deeply understanding and accepting reality as it is, rather than as one wishes it to be. (Ch 6)

**Idea Meritocracy (创意择优)** — Decision system where the best ideas win through radical truth, radical transparency, and credibility-weighted input. (Ch 3, 11)

**Lower-Level You (较低层次的你)** — The emotional, instinctive part of yourself driven by ego and subconscious reactions. (Ch 6)

**Machine Metaphor (机器比喻)** — Viewing yourself or your organization as a machine that can be designed, measured, and improved. (Ch 6, 19)

**Meaningful Relationships (有意义的人际关系)** — Genuine, caring connections where people truly support each other's success. (Ch 11, 13)

**Meaningful Work (有意义的工作)** — Work that people are passionate about and fully engaged in. (Ch 11, 13)

**Pain + Reflection = Progress (痛苦 + 反思 = 进步)** — Core learning loop: experience pain, reflect when calm, extract principle, apply to future. (Ch 3, 6)

**Radical Open-Mindedness (头脑极度开放)** — The practice of genuinely considering that you might be wrong and seeking out contrary views. (Ch 8)

**Radical Transparency (极度透明)** — Making almost all information visible to almost everyone in an organization. (Ch 12)

**Radical Truth (极度求真)** — Pursuing accurate understanding of reality regardless of comfort or convenience. (Ch 12)

**Refiner (改进者)** — Personality archetype: analyzes and improves ideas and processes. (Ch 9)

**Shaper (塑造者)** — Rare personality type that can go from vision to realization; combines vision, practicality, determination, and adaptability. (Ch 9)

**Systematic Decision-Making (系统化决策)** — Converting decision criteria into algorithms and using computers to process data and suggest actions. (Ch 4, 10)

**Thoughtful Disagreement (深思熟虑的意见分歧)** — The practice of exploring differences to find truth rather than to win arguments. (Ch 8)

**Tough Love (严厉之爱)** — Caring deeply for people while holding them to high standards and giving direct feedback. (Ch 11, 13)

**Two-Minute Rule (两分钟法则)** — Letting people speak for two minutes without interruption in discussions. (Ch 15)

**Two-Step Decision Process (两步决策流程)** — Learn first, then decide; gather and analyze information before forming conclusions. (Ch 10)

**Yield Curve Inversion (收益率曲线倒挂)** — When short-term interest rates exceed long-term rates, often signaling recession. (Ch 2)
